About


Web Developer

I started to take Web Development seriously in October 2021. Before this, I had built a few wordpress, squarespace and wix sites for friends and local companies. I had dabbled in Adobe Dreamweaver, HTML & CSS at a job in 2011, but there was a good 6-7 year gap until I touched any form of web design again. I re-sparked my interest upon leaving the military and doing a bit of freelance work in the transition, where I built some very basic sites and designed some logos and marketing material for a couple of local companies. From here, I mainly just dabbled with code and kept an eye on the tech industry whilst working as an electrician and smart home integrator.

Increasingly, as I worked on more and more smart home projects where I would be programming lighting systems and automating processes within the home, I realised that the technical, computer based work is what interested me the most, specifically when tailoring the UI and touch points for the customer. Naturally, I started self learning online and became extremely passionate about Web Development. I used resources such as Free Code Camp, The Odin Project & Udemy to further my skills and deep dive into a new industry that I wanted to know everything about.

The idea of a bootcamp interested me and I found the guys at {The Jump} - Digital School. I felt this was the most appropriate way for me to solidify my learning and get a foot in to the industry. This is where I am now and I will be finished with the course in August 2022.

Background

Military

I spent 6.5 years in the British Army as a Combat Engineer & Paratrooper. My relatively short, but fulfilling carrer is no doubt the most important period in my life to date. It was an experience that has moulded me to become the man I am today and I owe it everything. It instilled maturity, mental robustness and tenacity that I am not sure you can gain in any other line of work.

The experience I gained here is invaluable. During my time I completed arduous courses, multiple grueling exercises, was pushed to my limits regularly and deployed on an operational tour. Each individual experience provided me with transferrable skills that I use daily.
